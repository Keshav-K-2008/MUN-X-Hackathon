What Are the SDGs?

The Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are a universal call to action adopted by all United Nations member states in 2015. These 17 interconnected goals aim to create a better and more sustainable future for all by 2030.

Canada's Commitment

Canada has committed to implementing the SDGs both domestically and through international cooperation. EcoAction Canada focuses on four key goals where individual citizen action can make the most significant impact:

SDG 11: Sustainable Cities and Communities

Make cities and human settlements inclusive, safe, resilient and sustainable

Why It Matters

More than half of the world's population lives in cities, and this proportion is expected to increase to 68% by 2050. Making cities sustainable means creating career and business opportunities, safe and affordable housing, and building resilient societies and economies. Urban planning and management are crucial for sustainable development.

Canadian Context

Over 80% of Canadians live in urban areas. Cities like Vancouver, Toronto, and Montreal are implementing green infrastructure, improving public transit, and creating walkable neighborhoods. From bike lanes in Montreal to green buildings in Toronto, Canadian cities are leading sustainable urban development.

SDG 12: Responsible Consumption and Production

Ensure sustainable consumption and production patterns

Why It Matters

Our planet's resources are finite, yet global consumption and production drives economic growth. Sustainable consumption and production means doing more and better with less, reducing resource use, degradation, and pollution while increasing quality of life for all.

Canadian Context

Canadians generate approximately 720 kg of waste per person annually, one of the highest rates globally. However, provinces are implementing innovative recycling programs, extended producer responsibility, and circular economy initiatives. British Columbia's zero waste initiatives and Quebec's plastic bag bans demonstrate leadership in responsible consumption.

SDG 13: Climate Action

Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts

Why It Matters

Climate change is the defining challenge of our time, affecting every country on every continent. It is disrupting economies, costing lives, and impacting communities and countries today, with even worse consequences expected tomorrow. People are experiencing the significant impacts of climate change through changing weather patterns, rising sea levels, and more extreme weather events.

Canadian Context

Canada is warming twice as fast as the global average, with the Arctic warming even faster. From melting permafrost in the North to increased wildfires in British Columbia and flooding in Eastern provinces, climate impacts are already affecting Canadian communities. Canada has committed to net-zero emissions by 2050 and reducing emissions by 40-45% below 2005 levels by 2030.

SDG 17: Partnerships for the Goals

Strengthen the means of implementation and revitalize partnerships

Why It Matters

The SDGs can only be achieved through strong global partnerships and cooperation. Success will require inclusive partnerships built upon principles and values, a shared vision, and shared goals placing people and the planet at the center. Multi-stakeholder partnerships are needed to mobilize and share knowledge, expertise, technology, and financial resources.

Canadian Context

Canada recognizes that achieving the SDGs requires collaboration across all sectors of society. From government initiatives to grassroots movements, community organizations to corporate sustainability programs, Canadians are working together. EcoAction Canada itself exemplifies this goal by creating a platform that connects individual citizens with national sustainability objectives.
